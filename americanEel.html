American Eel

Season-Year-round

The American Eel is a primarily nocturnal eel. They can be found almost anywhere along the West Indies and all the way along the Atlantic and Gulf coasts from Greenland south, sometimes as far south as Brazil. They can be found inland in the United States from the Great Lakes, Mississippi Drainage and parts of Texas. They are common in lakes, streams or ponds closer to the coast but they prefer to hide under cover and out of the current. The American Eel is Connecticut's only species that spends most of its adult life in freshwater, but they spawn in salt water. All American Eels spawn in an area in the Western Atlantic Ocean called the Sargasso sea, which is the same sea as the Bermuda Triangle.

How to identify The American Eel

The American Eel has a long, flexible body with small eyes placed forward on its long head. They have small rounded pectoral fins.Small eels, known as glass eels, are nearly transparent, and are around 3 inches. Adults are typically a dark olive on its back and sides with a white-ish belly.They are normally 8 to 24 inches, with the State Survey maximum size being 41 inches. Conneticuts state record was 10.2 pounds and 52 inches long!
