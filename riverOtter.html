River Otter

River Otters are the largest member of the weasle family found in Connecticut. River otters can live in many types of aquatic habitats like streams, rivers, lakes, as well as freshwater and saltwater marshes. They prefer to live along marshes that have wooded lakes, or streams with pools and overhanging rocky banks.often they will use holes around the riverbank to make dens, but occasionally they will use dens that have been abandoned by beavers, muskrats or woodchucks instead. A River Otter's diet consists of an array of animals, including but not limited to fish, frogs, crayfish, shellfish, and sometimes aquatic insects, snakes, turtles, salamanders, earthworms, and small birds and mammals.

How to identify River Otters

River otters can be found in New England, parts of Canada, the Northwest, upper Great Lakes area,and Atlantic and Gulf Coast states. River Otters have slim bodys with short legs and a long muscular tail. River Otters can be 36 to 50 inches long and weigh between 12 and 25 pounds.The River Otters overall color is a dark brown which turns to gray on the underside. Otters have small heads with broad noses and small eyes and ears. They have long whickers and a short muzzle.Although otters have small legs, they have webbed feet to help them swim through the water, as well as sharp claws that help them catch prey.
